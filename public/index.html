Top 3 Focus Areas — High Impact
1
Improve team balance & keep rosters stable
Stop mid-season shuffles. Allow up to 3 retentions per team. Stable teams build better chemistry and deliver better results. Players want to belong to one team for the entire season.
2
Ensure equal playtime & rotations for every player
7 players per team is the structural fix. Captains and Wingmen to track rotations actively — every player should get at least 2–3 full games per event, not just the stronger ones.
3
Define clear rules for new players, swaps & transfers
Publish the process before Season 2 starts. Players want to know the rules in advance — not discover them mid-season. This builds trust and reduces disputes.

Referees for playoff & knockout games — the single most repeated request. Players are frustrated by disputes in high-stakes games without independent officiating
Maximum 7 players per team — 8 means some players sit too much. Bench time kills morale and discourages continued participation
No mid-season player transfers — destroys team identity and the chemistry players have invested in building together all season
Equal playtime rotation — captains must ensure every player gets 2–3 full games. Same players sitting repeatedly is demotivating and unfair
🟡 High Value — Players expect these changes
Auction / IPL-style draft — players are genuinely excited by this. Multiple responses called it out specifically as the right format — transparent, fair, competitive
Captains need more voice in team decisions — the committee should enable and support, not make all decisions without consulting captains
Player retention (up to 3 per team suggested) — allows chemistry to carry forward while still giving the league a fresh feel each season
Clear published rules for new players, swaps, and transfers — players want these in writing before Day 1, not communicated ad hoc
Points table system — one response suggested Winner 3pts, Runner-up 2pts, 3rd place 1pt per event, rather than win-only counting

Game Rules
⚖️ Referee's call is final. Only captains may address the referee. No other player may approach regarding any call.
12.1 Scoring
Games to 21, rally scoring. 20-20 → 2-point lead wins. 24-24 → first to 25 wins.
3 hits per team. No player hits twice in succession. Ball on line = in play.
Serve from behind baseline, hand only. Server joins play after serving.
12.2 Blocking
Defensive team may jump and block. A block does NOT count as one of the 3 hits.
12.3 Overreach & Attack — FVL Rule
Before attack is finished → hands off. After attack is finished → fair game to block, even over the net.
Attack = ball clearly going toward opponent's side — spike, pass, push, or tip.
1st, 2nd, or 3rd contact — all fair once attack is complete. Umpire has final say.
12.4 Violations
Foot fault on serve · touching net · ball under net · carrying the ball.
Serve cannot be blocked or spiked. Double contact = foul (except block+hit, or first single-action hit).
Center line: violation if body crosses AND interferes. Full crossing = foul regardless.
12.5 Rotation
Mandatory clockwise. Right-back serves first. Rotate after winning rally and regaining serve.
Back-row may attack from behind 10-foot line. Cannot attack/block above net in front of it.
12.7–12.8 Replacement & Tiebreaker
Replacement: similar skill level, not played that game or prior, accepted by both captains.
Tiebreaker: 1. Wins   2. Points differential   3. Coin toss. No tiebreaker games.

Season Transition — 4-Step Draft
1
Captain Selection
All 7 captains selected (4 Mini + 3 Nano). Auto-fill spot #1 — excluded from pool. Captain value deducted from budget before bidding begins.
2
Player Preference Declaration — 48 hours
Mini-available enters Mini draft (overflow → Nano). Nano-only skips Mini entirely. Both choices respected equally — no pressure either way.
3
Mini Auction Draft
Equal budget. Rotating nominations. Min bid 1 unit. Budget floor = 1 unit per remaining spot. Tier balance: max 1S + 1–2A + 2–3B per roster.
4
Nano Auction Draft
Nano-only pool + Mini overflow. Same auction format. Flexible tier balance — development focus.
Right to Match (RTM)

When another captain bids on a player from your prior team, you may match the winning bid and retain them. Recommended: 1 RTM per captain — 60-second window, same tier only.

Why RTM: No prior-season record keeping needed. Live bidding sets the price. Self-correcting — rivals who overbid drain their own budget.
Pending vote: 1 RTM per captain (budget pressure) or 3 RTM (chemistry focus)? 60-second window · same tier only.
