Game Rules
⚖️ Referee's call is final. Only captains may speak to the referee.
12.1 Scoring
Games to 21, rally scoring. 20-20 → 2-point lead wins. 24-24 → first to 25 wins.
3 hits per team. No player hits twice in succession. Ball on line = in play.
Serve behind baseline, hand only. Server joins play after serving.
12.2 Blocking
May jump and block. A block does NOT count as one of the 3 allowed hits.
12.3 Overreach & Attack — FVL Rule
Before attack is finished → hands off. After attack is finished → fair game to block, even over the net.
Attack = ball clearly going toward opponent's side (spike, pass, push, tip). 1st, 2nd, or 3rd contact — all fair.
Umpire has final discretion on whether attack was complete.
12.4 Violations
Foot fault on serve · ball in net not crossing · carrying · touching net · ball under net.
Serve cannot be blocked or spiked. Double contact = foul (except: block+hit, or first hit single action).
Center line: violation if body crosses AND interferes. Full body crossing = foul regardless.
12.5 Rotation · 12.7 Replacement · 12.8 Tiebreaker
Mandatory clockwise rotation. Right-back serves first. Rotate after winning rally.
Replacement: similar skill, not playing that game or prior, accepted by both captains.
Tiebreaker: 1. Wins 2. Points diff 3. Coin toss — no tiebreaker games played.

Season Transition — 4-Step Draft
1
Captain Selection
All 7 captains selected (4 Mini + 3 Nano). Auto-fill spot #1. Captain value deducted from budget before bidding.
2
Player Preference (48 hrs)
Mini-available enters Mini draft (overflow → Nano). Nano-only skips Mini entirely. Both respected equally.
3
Mini Auction Draft
Equal budget. Rotating nominations. Min bid 1 unit. Budget floor = 1 unit per remaining spot. Tier balance: max 1S + 1–2A + 2–3B.
4
Nano Auction Draft
Nano-only pool + Mini overflow. Same auction format. Flexible tier balance — development focus.
Right to Match (RTM)
When another captain bids on a player from your prior team, you may match the winning bid to retain them. Recommended: 1 RTM per captain — 60-second window, same tier only. Self-correcting: overbidding drains the rival's own budget.
