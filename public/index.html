Captains & Draft

Captain Eligibility

Not a core member:Core members are Wingmen only
Tier:A or S for Mini/Major. B acceptable for Nano.
Experience:At least one full FVL season
Attendance:Missed ≤2 sessions prior season
Conduct:No unresolved flags
Willingness:Must explicitly accept

Selection Process

1
Core members privately submit up to 2 nominations each.
2
Revealed simultaneously — candidates with 2+ advance.
3
Each discussed max 5 min; confirmed by majority vote.
4
Contacted within 24 hours to accept / decline.
5
If declined: next highest-voted player offered the role.
6
Captains announced together with Wingman pairings.

Season Transition — 4 Steps

1
Captain Selection
Core members select all 7 captains (4 Mini + 3 Nano). Captains auto-fill spot #1 — excluded from draft pool. Auction: captain value deducted from team budget before bidding.
2
Player Preference Declaration — 48 hours
Mini-available → enters Mini draft (overflow goes to Nano pool). Nano-only → skips Mini draft entirely. Both choices are respected equally.
3
Mini Auction Draft
4 Mini captains bid for 7 players each. Equal starting budget minus captain value. Tier balance enforced: max 1 Tier S, 1–2 Tier A, 2–3 Tier B per roster.
4
Nano Auction Draft
3 Nano captains draft from remaining pool (Nano-only + Mini overflow). Same auction format. Flexible tier balance.

💰 Auction Rules

Budget:Equal for all captains — amount TBC
Captain value:Assigned by core members; deducted pre-bidding
Nomination:Rotating order; any captain nominates any player
Min bid:1 unit per nomination
Budget floor:Keep ≥1 unit per remaining roster spot
Unsold:Assigned to nominating captain at 1 unit

🔄 Right to Match (RTM)

Recommended: 1 RTM per captain per draft.

When another captain bids on a player from your prior team, you may match the winning bid to retain them.

Why RTM: No prior-season record keeping. Live bidding sets the price. Self-correcting.

Parameters (pending vote): 1 RTM · 60-second window · Same tier only.

Game Rules

⚖️ Referee's call is final. Only captains may speak to the referee. No other players may approach regarding any call.
12.1 Scoring
Games to 21, rally scoring. Point: ball lands in opponent's half, or opponent fails to return / hits net.
20-20: play until 2-point lead. 21-21: 2-point lead wins. 24-24: first to 25 wins.
Serve from behind baseline, hand only. Server joins play after serving.
3 hits per team before returning. No player may hit twice in succession.
Ball on boundary line = in play.
12.2 Blocking
Defensive team may jump and attempt to block. Ball in opponent's half → blocking team scores. Ball out → opposing team scores.
A block does NOT count as one of the 3 allowed hits.
12.3 Overreach & Attack — FVL Rule
Before attack is finished → hands off.
After attack is finished → fair game to block, even over the net.
Attack is finished when ball is clearly travelling toward opponent's side — spike, pass, push, or tip.
1st, 2nd, or 3rd contact — all fair game once attack is complete.
Umpire has final discretion. If not directed toward the other side → overreaching is a violation.
12.4 Violations
Stepping over court line when serving / serving from inside court.
Ball hits net and fails to cross — violation. Hits net and crosses — legal.
Carrying the ball.
Touching net with any body part. Exception: net contacts player — not a violation.
Ball travels under the net.
Serve cannot be blocked or spiked.
During serve, ball touches ceiling or equipment above.
Ball touches roof and reaches other court — foul. On 1st/2nd pass — team may continue.
Body contact: allowed only if ball is bounced, not rolled over.
12.4a–c Contact Rules
Double Contact
Hitting ball twice in a row — foul. Exception: block then hit — allowed. Exception: first hit in single action — allowed.
Simultaneous Contact
Ball touches different body parts simultaneously — always allowed. Not a double contact.
Center Line
Violation if body crosses AND interferes. Entire body crossing = foul regardless of interference.
12.5 Rotation
Mandatory. Clockwise. Right-back serves first. Rotate clockwise after winning rally.
Players must be in correct position before serve. Error → other team earns point and serve.
Back-row players may attack from behind 10-foot line. Cannot attack/block above net from in front of it.
12.7 Player Replacement
Injury → replacement from roster not playing that game or the immediately prior game.
Similar skill — not highest available. Must be accepted by both captains.
12.8 Standings Tiebreaker
1st: WinsTeam with more wins ranks higher
2nd: Points DifferentialTotal scored minus total conceded
3rd: Coin Toss / RandomisationNo tiebreaker games played
