What's Working Well
Communications & scheduling rated highest (4.71) — players feel well-informed and organised throughout the season
Fun + competitive matches — the format works; games are engaged and contested at all levels
Strong organisation & volunteers — players explicitly recognise the effort core members put in
Real skill growth — 82% (28/34) feel their game has improved since joining FVL Mini
100% fee satisfaction — every respondent felt the fee provides value for money
Top 3 Focus Areas — High Impact
1
Improve team balance & keep rosters stable
Stop mid-season shuffles. Allow up to 3 retentions. Stable teams build better chemistry. Players want to belong to one team for the full season.
2
Ensure equal playtime & rotations for every player
7 players per team is the structural fix. Every player gets 2–3 full games minimum. Captains and Wingmen actively track rotations.
3
Define clear rules for new players, swaps & transfers
Publish the process before Season 3 starts. Players need to know the rules upfront — not discover them mid-season.
Detailed Feedback
🔴 Must Address — Highest frequency
Referees for playoff & knockout games — most repeated request. Players frustrated by disputes in high-stakes games without independent officiating
Maximum 7 players per team — 8 means bench time kills morale and discourages continued participation
No mid-season player transfers — destroys team identity and chemistry players have built
Equal playtime rotation — captains must ensure every player gets 2–3 full games. Same players sitting repeatedly is demotivating
🟡 High Value — Players expect these
Auction / IPL-style draft — multiple responses called it out specifically. Transparent, fair, competitive
Captains need more voice — committee should enable and support, not make all decisions
Player retention up to 3 per team — chemistry carries forward while still refreshing the pool
